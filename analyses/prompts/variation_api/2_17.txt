Using NHANES data from 2011-2016, we will do a weighted logistic regression using exam weights.
We will include participants whose age at screening is 20 years or older.
We will also exclude participants with incomplete any of the following PHQ-9 quesitons:  "have little interest in doing things", "feeling down, depressed, or hopeless", "trouble sleeping or sleeping too much", "feeling tired or having little energy", "poor appetite or overeating", "feeling bad about yourself", "trouble concentrating on things", "moving or speaking slowly or too fast", "thought you would be better off dead".
We will also exclude participants who were pregnant at the time of the exam and exclude participants with incomplete any of the following demographic data: age at screening, gender, race/Hispanic origin.
Additionally, we will exclude participants with incomplete all of following diabetes exam or survey data: self-report of previous diagnosis of diabetes by medical professionals, fasting plasma glucose (FPG) (mg/dL), glycosylated hemoglobin (HbA1c) (%), post 2-hour plasma glucose after 75 g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)(mg/dl), whether they take glucose lowering agents, whether they take insulin.
Covariates will include age at screening categorized to 20-39, 40-59, 60-79, equal or more than 80, gender, race/Hispanic origin categorized into White, Black, Mexican Hispanic, Other Hispanic, Asian, others.
Our predictor is depression outcome, which is based on the total score from the nine questions were calculated where each question has value of Not at all (0), Several days (1), More than half the days (2), Nearly every day (3), and all were added and categorized as None [0-4], Mild [5-9], Moderate [10-14], Moderately Severe [15-19], and Severe [20 or higher]): 
The outcome will be diabetes presence where participants who met one of the following were considered to have diabetes and else as no: fasting plasma glucose (FPG) (mg/dL) ≥126 mg/dL, glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) %  ≥6.5%, post 2-hour plasma glucose after 75 g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)(mg/dl) ≥200 mg/dL, self-report of previous diagnosis of diabetes by medical professionalstaking glucose-lowering agents, taking insulin.
