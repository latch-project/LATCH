We plan to conduct a logistic regression analysis to examine the relationship between serum albumin (g/dL) and diabetic retinopathy using NHANES data from the years 2011 to 2020. 
The study will include all individuals whose age in years at screening was 30 years or older who have complete data on both serum albumin (g/dL) and diabetes affected eyes/had retinopathy.
Participants will be excluded if their age in years at screening is less than 30 years old, or age when first told the patient had diabetes was before age 30, or had uncertain diabetes status. 
Uncertainty is based on their answer to the question “Has a doctor ever told you that you have diabetes?” and if they answer as "borderline" and  either have a missing glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) value or an glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) below 6.5%. 
We will also exclude anyone with missing data on serum albumin or diabetes affected eyes/had retinopathy.
The main predictor in the analysis will be serum albumin (g/dL), categorized into quartiles(%) Q1: <25, Q2: ≥25 and <50, Q3: ≥50 and <75, Q4: ≥75. 
The outcome variable will be the presence or absence of diabetic retinopathy, based on responses to the question “diabetes affected eyes had retinopathy?”, coded as 1 for "Yes" and 0 for "No".
We will adjust the analysis for potential confounding factors including gender, age in years at screening, and race/Hispanic origin, which will be categorized as White, Black, Mexican American, or Other.
