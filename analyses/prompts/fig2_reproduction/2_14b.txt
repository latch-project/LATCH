This study will examine the relationship between glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) (%) levels and cognitive performance using a weighted linear regression analysis.
The analysis will use NHANES data from the 2011-2014 period. The study population is limited to participants whose age in years at screening is 60 years and older. 
Individuals will be excluded if they have missing data for any of the specified cognitive assessments: digit symbol substitution test (DSST), animal fluency score, and CERAD Score Trial 1 recall, CERAD Score Trial 2 recall, CERAD Score Trial 3 recall, and delayed word recall tasks. 
Furthermore, participants with missing data on doctor told you have diabetes, glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) (%), or fasting plasma glucose (FPG) (mg/dL) will be excluded.
The primary outcome of interest is delayed word recall and the predictor is glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) percentage. 
The analysis will adjust for several covariates: age (categorized as <70 or ≥70 years), gender, and race/Hispanic origin (categorized as Non-Hispanic White, Black, Non-Hispanic Asian, Mexican-American, and others).
