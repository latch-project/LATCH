This study aims to investigate the association between the Weight-Adjusted Waist Index (WWI) and all-cause mortality using a weighted Cox proportional hazards regression model we will be using exam weights.
The analysis will utilize NHANES data collected between 2015-2016.
The study population will include participants whose age in years at screening is 20 years and older who have either diabetes or prediabetes. 
Diabetes is defined as meeting any of the following conditions: doctor told them they have diabetes, taking insulin now, take diabetic pills to lower blood sugar, a fasting blood glucose (FBG) (mg/dL) of 126 mg/dL or higher, or a glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) (%) of 6.5 or greater. 
Prediabetes is defined as meeting all of the following criteria: ever told have prediabetes, an fasting blood glucose (FBG) (mg/dL) level between 100 and 126 mg/dL, and glycohemoglobin (HbA1c) (%) equal or more than 5.7 and less than 6.5%. 
Individuals will be excluded if they are missing the necessary data to calculate the WWI, which are waist circumference (cm) and  weight (kg) or mortality status or  months followup examination
The primary predictor for this analysis is the Weight-Adjusted Waist Index (WWI), calculated as waist circumference (cm) divided by the square root of weight (kg). This index will be categorized into quartiles  Q1: <=25%, Q2: >25 and <=50, Q3: >50 and <=75%, Q4: >75%.
The outcome months followup examination and mortality data. The model will be adjusted for several covariates, including age in years at screening, gender, and race/Hispanic origin (categorized as Mexican American, Other Hispanic, Non-Hispanic White, Non-Hispanic Black, and Other race).
